Keep a Breast

From The Giant: The Definitive Obey Giant Site

In 2005, the Keep a Breast Foundation worked with numerous artists to create art on casts of women's breasts. Shepard created and stenciled a cast of his wife Amanda, who at the time, was eight months pregnant with their daughter Vivian. Shepard has long been a supporter of the the Keep a Breast Foundation. The casts were auctioned off on eBay in October of 2005, with all proceeds benefitting the Keep a Breast Youth Awareness Program.

From Keep a Breast.org:

"Margaret Kilgallen, one of my favorite artists, died of breast cancer. Both of my Mom's sisters died of breast cancer, and my mom had preventative surgery just in case. I think breasts are lovely and their health, as well as their owner's health, is very important to me. I appreciate both the form and function of breasts, especially since my wife and I had our daughter Vivienne whose health and happiness depends on quality time with boobs. Oh yeah, my happiness depends on quality time with boobs, too. I think it's only fair for me to give back after all that breasts have given me."

- Shepard Fairey
